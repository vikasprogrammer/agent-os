What Agent OS gives you

Not a chatbot. A place to actually run agents.

Six things a team needs before autonomous agents can do real work — and one of them, not the whole story, is the gate that keeps them in bounds.

Fleet

Stand up agents, watch or walk away

Spin up agents for support, ops, coding, research — headed when you want to look over their shoulder, headless when you don't. Each runs in its own session you can drop into live from the browser.

Memory

They remember, and they compound

Every agent keeps a persistent memory instead of starting cold each time. A recurring reflection pass turns what the fleet did — the wins and the friction — into lessons the whole team inherits.

Knowledge

One shared brain to draw from

A living knowledge base and a shared task queue that people and agents write to together. Hand off a task; an agent picks it up, does the work, and closes the loop — all on one board.

Chat

Reachable where you already talk

@mention an agent in Slack or Discord and it runs as you, in the thread, with your permissions — no public URL, no new app to learn. Follow-ups stay in the same conversation with full context.

Governance

Every real action stays in bounds

A send, a charge, a deploy — each one passes through a single gate: your policy classifies it, a human approves what's risky, budget debits, identity signs it, and audit records it. Autonomy you can put in production.

Evolution

They build and improve themselves

Agents draft new reusable skills, refine their own instructions, and delegate work to each other — every change reversible, every change on the record. The fleet you run next month is sharper than today's.
